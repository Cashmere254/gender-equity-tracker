Angaza Programme — 2024 Annual Impact Report

Participant Narratives:

Wanjiru received a multi-year comprehensive scholarship to attend high school in Nyeri County.
Coming from an arid and semi-arid region, she had been at risk of dropping out due to financial
constraints. After joining the Angaza programme, she reports feeling significantly more confident
in her academic abilities. She now leads the school debate club and advocates for girls' education
in her community.

Amina completed the Career Activation workshop and attended a career symposium in Nairobi.
She has set clear goals for pursuing university education in engineering. Her mentor visits
monthly to support her academic journey and she has secured a scholarship application for
tertiary education abroad.

Fatuma was enrolled in the Manyatta Homeschooling programme in Samburu County. Before
joining, she was out of school and lacked basic literacy skills. After one year she has returned
to formal schooling, passed her literacy assessment, and reports that she now feels her rights
to education are being upheld by her community.

The programme has awarded 223 multi-year scholarships to high-achieving girls at risk of
leaving school. Over 509 girls have returned to school through the Manyatta component.
83.6 percent of Angaza graduates transition to universities and TVETs to further their education.
Girls report increased confidence, stronger leadership skills, and a sense of agency over
their personal and professional futures.
