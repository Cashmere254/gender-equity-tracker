Washa Programme — 2024 Annual Impact Report

Participant Narratives:

Njeri participated in the Emerging Leaders Institute and developed a Social Action Project
focused on menstrual health awareness in her community in Machakos. She received a seed
grant of KES 50,000 to implement the project. She reports that the feminist leadership
training gave her the tools to organise, advocate, and build a grassroots movement.
Her project has now reached over 300 young women and girls.

Zawadi joined the Washa programme from the social enterprise sector. The strategic visioning
workshops helped her clarify her organisation's mission and build a network of like-minded
women leaders. She now leads the We For She movement, one of four grassroots movements
born out of Washa leadership development initiatives.

An organisational development assessment was completed with a women-led NGO in Mombasa.
The process identified capacity gaps in financial management and communications, and
provided targeted training to strengthen institutional systems.

The Washa programme has enhanced the leadership skills of 730 young women from across
the social enterprise, student leadership, and community development sectors. A total of
$124,025 has been invested in seed grants to propel social action initiatives. Four grassroots
movements have been created: We For She, Flourishing Girls, Feminist Evolve, and Shimba Hills Rise.
