Urithi Programme — 2024 Annual Impact Report

Participant Narratives:

Aisha attended the Leadership Academy intensive workshop hosted at her partner school.
She reports that the sessions on sexual and reproductive health rights gave her information
she had never accessed before. She now leads the Akili Dada Club at her school and
mentors junior students on girls' rights and personal safety.

Grace was connected to a female mentor through the Urithi mentorship network. Her mentor,
a professional working in the finance sector, visits the school monthly and has inspired
Grace to pursue a career in accounting. Grace says the mentorship relationship has given
her the confidence to assert herself and navigate high school challenges.

The community convening brought together 40 parents, educators, and community leaders
to address gender barriers and discuss the safety of girls in the region. Participants
reported greater awareness of girls' rights and committed to supporting female education
in their communities. Social change projects initiated by participants reached over 40,000
community members.

Urithi currently operates across 24 active partner schools. Over 10,000 girls have been
linked to mentors through school clubs and Leadership Academies. More than 50,000 girls
have been indirectly impacted through mentoring activities and advocacy outreach.
